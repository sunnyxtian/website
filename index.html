What I'm reading now

picture from penguin random house

Chain Gang All Stars by Nana Kwame Adjei-Brenyan

A part sci fi, part social commentary on the prison industrial system, racism, and sports entertainment. Our main character is a modern day gladiator and it gets emotional. Though this is fiction, Nana inserts a few citations in each chapter that show how the book was inspired by the reality of the US prison system.

picture from amazon

Case Closed (Manga series) by Gosho Aoyama

Sometimes I like to look at pictures instead of reading a bunch of words. Manga is great for that. Each volume has a few bite sized mysteries that the protagonist, Conan Edogawa, solves, even though he is only in first grade (or is he??). I'm only on volume 4 and I'm already hooked. Great for fans of mystery novels and manga!

Recent reads

picture from amazon

Ghost Wall by Sarah Moss

The plot: Sulevia is a teen girl forced by her family to participate in a university experiment where they live like they are in Stone Age Britain. This family is basically boiling over with tension the entire time. Sulevia's father is controlling and infuriatingly misogynistic, and Sulevia starts to find her voice against him with the help of an older female uni student.

picture from amazon

Human Acts by Han Kang

This book was actually devastating. It is a fictionalized account of a real boy who was murdered during the 1980 student protests in Gwangju, Korea. If you want to be mad at the government and also cry, read this?

picture from amazon

Nichijou Volume 1 by Keiichi Arawa

Usually, when I start reading a new manga, I have no idea what's going on, but that feeling goes away after getting to know the characters, plot, etc. When I read this, though, I was utterly confused the whole way through, and I don't know if that's a good or bad thing.
